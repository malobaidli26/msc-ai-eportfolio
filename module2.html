Module 2

This page will document my learning, activities, artefacts, and reflections for Module 2.

Module Overview

Unit 1 – Introduction to Artificial Intelligence

Learning Outcomes

  • Understand the concept of Artificial Intelligence and its origins.
  • Develop awareness of the application areas and capabilities of AI systems.
  • Understand the ethical and governance issues around AI.

Learning Activities

  • Reviewed the lecturecast introducing the origins, definitions, and evolution of Artificial Intelligence.
  • Studied the role of AI in society and modern organisations.
  • Explored ethical, safety, and governance issues related to AI systems.

Unit 2 – Artificial Intelligence: The Benefits and Threats to Business

Learning Outcomes

  • Develop awareness of ethical issues surrounding AI systems.
  • Evaluate the benefits and challenges of AI technologies in business environments.

Learning Activities

  • Reviewed how AI technologies support business operations and decision-making.
  • Analysed the advantages of AI such as automation, efficiency, and data-driven insights.
  • Explored challenges including ethical concerns, bias, and regulatory requirements.
  • Studied AI governance frameworks and ethical standards.

Artefacts

AI in Business: Benefits and Challenges Analysis

This activity examined how Artificial Intelligence is used in business environments, focusing on both its benefits and associated risks. It analysed how organisations can leverage AI technologies to improve efficiency while also addressing ethical and regulatory challenges.

Meeting Notes

This unit was completed through guided reading and independent analysis. The focus was on understanding how AI systems are applied in business and how organisations must balance innovation with ethical responsibility.

Reflection

This unit helped me understand that while AI offers significant benefits to businesses, it also introduces risks that must be carefully managed. I learned the importance of ethical frameworks and regulatory standards in ensuring that AI systems are used responsibly. This strengthened my ability to evaluate AI technologies not only from a technical perspective but also from a business and ethical standpoint.

Artefacts

Introductory AI Learning Notes

This activity introduced the foundations of Artificial Intelligence, including its origins, key definitions, industrial applications, and ethical considerations. It provided the background needed to understand AI as both a technical and professional discipline.

Seminar Notes

The introductory seminar explained the module structure, assessments, learning expectations, and the role of practical engagement throughout the module. It also provided an opportunity to clarify module requirements and understand how AI concepts will be developed across the following units.

Reflection

This unit helped me understand Artificial Intelligence as a broad field that combines technical capability, ethical responsibility, and professional practice. I learned that AI is not only about automation or intelligent systems, but also about responsible design, governance, and awareness of societal impact. This unit provided the foundation for the rest of the module.

Module 2 builds on the foundations of computing and explores more advanced topics in artificial intelligence, data analysis, and enterprise systems.
