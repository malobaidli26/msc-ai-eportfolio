Module 1 – Launch into Computing

This page documents my learning activities, artefacts, and reflections developed during Module 1 of the MSc Artificial Intelligence programme at the University of Essex Online.

Unit 1 – What is Computing?

Learning Outcomes

  • Identify and critically analyse computing challenges in business systems.
  • Understand computing concepts through research and analysis.
  • Recognise the legal, ethical, and professional aspects of computing.

Artefacts

Case Study: AI-Powered Assistants in Business Transformation

This case study analysed how AI-powered assistants are transforming business operations by improving efficiency, automation, and decision-making processes.

Evidence: View Assignment

Discussion Activity

I participated in a discussion exploring the impact of computing technologies on modern business systems and shared insights on how artificial intelligence can support organisational transformation.

Meeting Notes

Feedback and interaction for this unit occurred through the online discussion forum and peer responses.

Reflection

This unit helped me understand the broader role of computing in organisations and society. It also strengthened my ability to analyse how emerging technologies such as artificial intelligence influence business transformation.

Unit 2 – Logical Foundations of Computing

Learning Outcomes

  • Understand Boolean logic and set theory in computing.
  • Analyse how logical operations support computing systems.
  • Apply logic tools such as truth tables and logic gates.

Artefacts

Case Study: Boolean Logic in Search Engines and Digital Advertising

This case study explored how Boolean logic supports information retrieval systems such as search engines and digital advertising algorithms.

Evidence: View Assignment

Logic Circuit Design: Practical Implementation of Boolean Algebra

This activity involved designing a simple logical circuit demonstrating how Boolean algebra can be applied in computational problem-solving.

Reflection

Through this unit I gained a stronger understanding of how logical structures underpin computing systems and how Boolean algebra is applied in real-world digital platforms.
